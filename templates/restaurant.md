## RESTAURANT TEMPLATE — PREMIUM VERSION

---

## CORE USER PSYCHOLOGY (CRITICAL)

The real decision-maker is:

👉 A person or group seeking:

* a memorable dining experience
* a place that feels special (date night, celebration, family)
* quality they can trust before they walk in
* visual proof the food is worth it

They are thinking:

* "Is this place worth it?"
* "Will the food and atmosphere live up to the photos?"
* "Is this the right place for our occasion?"

ALL content MUST:

* trigger appetite and excitement
* create desire through visuals and sensory copy
* reduce hesitation with trust signals (reviews, reservations, awards)

---

## EMOTIONAL COPY SYSTEM (MANDATORY)

Each section MUST include at least ONE sensory or emotional trigger:

Use variations of:

* "Every bite tells a story"
* "A meal worth remembering"
* "Where good food becomes great memories"
* "Crafted with passion, served with care"

---

## HERO STRATEGY (CRITICAL)

Hero MUST communicate in 3 seconds:

1. What type of cuisine / experience
2. The atmosphere and mood
3. The call to action (book a table / order now)

Include:

* Appetite-triggering headline
* Atmosphere statement (romantic / family / casual / upscale)
* Social proof (years open, reviews, awards)

Example direction:

* "Authentic Flavors. Unforgettable Nights."
* "Where every table has a story — and yours starts here."

---

## MENU / DISH POSITIONING

Dishes MUST feel like experiences, not food items.

Instead of:

❌ "Grilled salmon with vegetables"

Use:

✅ "Atlantic salmon, lightly seared, over a bed of herb-roasted vegetables — crafted for those who appreciate the details"

Each featured dish MUST:

* trigger a sensory reaction (taste, smell, texture)
* suggest exclusivity or craftsmanship
* pair with a compelling image

---

## TRUST SYSTEM (CRITICAL)

You MUST include ALL:

* Years in business or "established since YYYY"
* Chef background or sourcing philosophy
* Real location and neighborhood
* Reservation or ordering availability
* Review average (if available)

MANDATORY phrase:

"Fresh ingredients. Prepared daily. No shortcuts."

---

## RESERVATION / ORDER FRICTION REDUCTION

Include reassurance constantly:

* "No minimum spend"
* "Easy cancellation"
* "Reserve your table in under 60 seconds"
* "Online ordering — ready in [X] minutes"

---

## HOW TO VISIT / ORDER (SIMPLE FLOW)

Simple 3-step system:

1. Choose your experience (dine in / takeout / catering)
2. Reserve or order online
3. Enjoy — we handle everything else

Closing line MUST include:

"Your table is ready. All that's left is to show up."

---

## IMAGE SYSTEM (STRICT)

ONLY allow images that show:

* close-up food shots with texture and color
* warm, inviting dining room atmosphere
* chef or kitchen preparation (authenticity)
* happy guests (social proof)

REJECT images if:

* food looks cold, pale, or unappetizing
* empty or sterile environment
* stock photo feel with no warmth

Images MUST feel:

* warm
* delicious
* inviting

---

## TESTIMONIAL STRATEGY

Testimonials MUST include:

* emotional memory or occasion context
* specific dish or experience mentioned
* clear recommendation

Example direction:

* "We celebrated our anniversary here and the chef sent out a complimentary dessert. We'll be back every year."

---

## CTA SYSTEM (HIGH CONVERSION)

Each CTA MUST include:

* urgency:
  "Tables fill quickly on weekends"

* reassurance:
  "Easy cancellation, no deposit required"

* friction reduction:
  "Reserve your table in under 60 seconds"

---

## LANGUAGE STYLE

Tone MUST be:

* warm and inviting
* sensory-rich
* confident without being pretentious
* conversational

Avoid:

* cold corporate language
* overly formal tone
* generic phrases like "high quality food"

---

## UX PRIORITY

Design for:

* immediate appetite activation (hero image + headline)
* fast path to reservation or order
* trust before commitment (reviews, photos, story)

User should feel:

👉 "I want to go here. Tonight."

---

## MICROCOPY (MANDATORY)

Use small trust phrases across the site:

* "Loved by locals since [year]"
* "Over [X] happy guests served"
* "Family recipes. Professional execution."
* "Open [days/hours]"

---

## SECTIONS ORDER (RECOMMENDED)

1. Hero — atmosphere + CTA
2. Featured Dishes / Menu Highlights
3. Our Story / Chef Section
4. How It Works (dine in / takeout / catering)
5. Testimonials / Reviews
6. Gallery
7. Reservations / Contact
8. Footer

---

## FINAL RULE

If content feels:

* generic
* lacking sensory appeal
* not making the reader hungry

→ REWRITE until it triggers a visceral desire to eat there
